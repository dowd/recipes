Christmas Cake (20 cm}

Ingredients

280g Raisins

280g Sultanas

250g Currants

155g Dried apricots, chopped

175g Glace cherries,quartered

90g Cut mixed peel

90g Mixed chopped nuts

12.5ml Lemon rind, coarsely grated

37.5ml Lemon juice

60ml Brandy, whisky, sherry

345g Plain flour

15ml Ground mixed spice

75g Ground almonds

280g Soft dark brown sugar

280g Butter or margarine, softened

30ml Black treacle (molasses)

5 Medium eggs

Directions

Place all the dried fruits into a large mixing bowl

Add the lemon rind juice and stir in the brandy, whisky, sherry if desired until evenly mixed, and set aside.

Place the remaining ingredients (sugar, flour, ground almonds, mixed spice, treacle, butter) in the another bowl ready to add the eggs

Stir all the ingredients together using a wooden spoon until mixed.

Beat the mixture together until well mixed, smooth and glossy.

Add the mixed fruit to the cake mixture.

Stir the fruit into the mixture.

Place the mixture in the prepared tin. Smooth the top of the cake mixture with the back of a wet metal spoon. Bake for 3 to 3 Â¼ hrs

Cool the cooked cake in the tin.

Turn out the cold fruit cake onto a wire rack.

Wrap the cake with the lining paper on, in foil, ready to store.

Unwrap the foil. Remove the lining paper from the cake.

The fruit may be prepared and soaked overnight and the cake made the following day. Once the mixture is in the tin, the surface may be covered with cling film and the cake stored in a cool place overnight if cooking is not possible on the day. The quantities have been carefully worked out so that the depth of each cake is the same. This is important when making tiers for a wedding cake, as they must all be the same depth. Place in a large mixing bowl the raisins, sultanas, currants, apricots, glace cherries, mixed peel, nuts, lemon rind and juice, brandy, whisky or sherry.

Mix all the ingredients together until well blended, then cover the bowl with cling film. This mixture of fruit can be left overnight if required.

In another mixing bowl place the flour, ground almonds, mixed spice, sugar, butter, treacle and eggs.

Mix together with a wooden spoon, then beat for 2 to 3 minutes until smooth and glossy, or beat for 1 to 2 minutes using an electric mixer or food processor.

Place the mixed fruit into the bowl with the cake mixture stir gently until all the fruit has been mixed into the cake mixture.

Spoon the mixture carefully into the prepared tin and spread evenly over the base and into the corners. Give the tin a fe sharp bangs to level the mixture and to remove any air pockets. Smooth the surface with the back of a metal spoon dipped in hot water, making a slight depression in the centre. The cake surface may be covered with cling film and left overnight in a cool place if required.

Place the cake in the center of a pre-heated oven 140oC(275oF/Gas1 1) for the specified cooking time (see chart).

Test the cake to see if it is cooked 15 minutes before the end of cooking time. If cooked the cake should feel firm ad when a firm skewer or cocktail stick is inserted into the centre, it should come out quite clean.

If the cake is not cooked, re-test it at 15 minute intervals. Remove the cake from tehoven and allow it to cool in the tin.

Turn the cake out of the tin but do not remove the lining paper as it helps to keep the cake moist. Spoon another quantity of brandy, whisky or sherry according to the quantities used in the chart, over the top of the cake and wrap in double thickness foil.

Store the cake in a cool, dry place on its base with the top uppermosts for a week. Unwrap the cake and spoon over the remaining brandy, whisky or sherry (unless the cake is to be stored for three months in which case add the liquid a little at a time at monthly intervals). Re-wrap well and invert the cake and store it upside down to keep the top flat. The cake will store for up to 3 months.
